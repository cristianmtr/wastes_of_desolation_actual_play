A Mourning

Thyel and Arthur arrive back in Corum. They are aghast with the discovery of Larona's secret affair with Witfar, son of the murderer of their other friend, Rose.

They feel lost and directionless, and seek the solace of their companions' circle, but it feels broken. Its magic has been lost with the loss of two of their dearest. They gather in the suite above the tavern and mourn their loss, the first time they are all together in a long time. Since the cursed bairam at the Vilf's manor up north.

Oracles

Random descriptors: Fiercely, Interesting

Arthur is fascinated by the feelings he's experiencing, and tries to capture this in his journal. The aggregation of the depressive energies in the room bring forth memories of ancient forces that feed upon such desperation. The others notice him and, while it is inappropriate that he find this inspiring, they are empathetic. Another of the group plays a tragic song on their flute. Thyel starts to hum along with the popular tragedy's melody. Soon they all find themselves soothed and brought closer together in this dark time.

Back in the barrow

The morning Arthur and Thyel make plans to return to the fields to the west, where they discovered the burial mounds. It seemed to them like priceless jewels could be found there. They needed all they money they could stash to get out of this cursed land, filled with sorrowful memories and ghosts of their past mistakes. After a quick check of their equipment and a short round about the market for rations, they are back on the trails.

As the two approach the mound to descend upon the unwary creatures, they hear the movement stop underneath, in the dug-out hole. Suddenly, out of the hole two wolf-men jump out, followed by two more.

Two of the wolf abominations jumps us, scratching both Thyel and Arthur on their chests, before the two manage to push them away and gain their footing. Arthur's spells's strength is missing, probably from the surprise of the attack. The fire fizzles out. However, he still manages to hurl a small orb of melting acid at the pack leader's disgusting face.

Thyel lashes at the pack leader, pinning it to the ground and stabbing it in its shoulders and chest with fury and great accuracy. The thing shoves Thyel away, but Thyel lands next to one of the other fiends, slashing its forearm deeply.

But he has exposed his back, and one of the other creatures bites his shoulder and jerks him. He gets out by knocking it in the stomach with his knee, but he is bleeding from the shoulder now.

The leader sees this opening and lunges at him. Thyel is ready and pierces the creature's throat with his short sword. It goes limp and falls to the dirt.

The others, seeing their leader lifeless, squeal and dash for the dark forests.

They rest.
