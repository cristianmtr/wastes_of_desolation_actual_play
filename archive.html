This is a solo actual play I am starting. I am using World of Dungeons right now, but I will experiment with others. I will be importing liberally from my collection of RPG books, as the mood strikes me.

Lines in this formatting will be out of character, out of fiction. Will include things like rolls, basic interpretation of some rolls, rough combat logs. These can be skipped.

These lines are in-fiction. They will describe the world and actions in a literary way, similar to a novel, to the best of my ability.

I am going for a Dying Earth + Cthulhu + Numenera setting. But again, I will add whatever fits my fancy. Hope you enjoy this!

Session 0

This is a world in the far future. Civilizations have risen and collapsed. So many that history has forgotten them. Indeed, most people don't have the knowledge.

The world exists now as a wasteland scattered with the light of civilizations. Most of these are organized on their own principles. Connections are frail.

The people living are aware of their limited and fragile state. There is a weariness constant across the populations. Most people prefer to fill in their time with pleasures, excesses, and reckless adventures. A biological ticking clock in our guts tells us that all will perish soon. We might as well rejoice in the moments left.

But there are organizations and leaders that still attempt to bring humanity together. They intuit the ferocious threats: the ancient, mysterious makers of our worlds.

City and setting expansion

This city is called Corum. It is a large bustling mess of a city. It is located by a great ocean, in the northern regions. The layout is scattered and meandering, built with layers upon layers, as history and the people deemed fit. The architecture is eclectic and eerie, with a foundation of strong stone and wood, but with some dwellings made of alien materials. These are the houses of the sorcerors, or of the awed rich mercenary travelers. It is unknown why their buildings were built so. Some say these men brought these materials from their fabled journeys; some say the materials were warped by their very presence.
